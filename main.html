Architecture Through the Ages

Classical Architecture

Classical architecture, originating in ancient Greece and Rome, established the foundational principles of Western architectural design. Characterized by its emphasis on proportion, symmetry, and the use of classical orders (Doric, Ionic, and Corinthian), this style has influenced building design for over two millennia. The Greeks' focus on temple architecture and the Romans' innovations in engineering and urban planning continue to inspire modern architects and urban designers.

Modern Architecture

Modern architecture emerged in the early 20th century as a response to rapid technological advancement and changing social needs. This style emphasizes functionality, clean lines, and the integration of new materials like steel, glass, and reinforced concrete. Notable pioneers like Le Corbusier, Frank Lloyd Wright, and Mies van der Rohe championed the principle that "form follows function," creating buildings that were both aesthetically striking and highly practical.
